Direct Deposit Bank Information
How would you like to receive your ANCHOR benefit payment?  
Direct Deposit   Check
In order to comply with banking rules, answer the following question:
Will the funds be deposited into an account outside of the United States?       Yes No  
If you choose direct deposit, enter the bank routing number and your account number below. Also select the type of account (checking or savings).
The routing number is the 9-digit number at the bottom left of your check and your account number is to the right of the routing number. See Sample Check for an image showing the bank routing and account numbers on a check. The bank routing number must contain 9 numbers. Your bank account number must have a minimum length of 4 characters with only the first position containing either a letter or number. Contact your bank or financial institution if you have any questions regarding these numbers.
The Division of Taxation is not responsible for a lost benefit if you enter the wrong account information. Contact your financial institution to get the correct routing and account numbers and to make sure it will accept your direct deposit. Do not use the routing number on a deposit slip if it is different from the routing number on your checks.
